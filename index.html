About Me

My passion for computer science ignited during my years at UCLA, where I pursued an undergraduate degree in Biology. While studying Biology, I took a series of 'Calculus for Life Sciences' courses, where I used R for mathematical modeling in biological systems. It was during this time that I discovered the fascinating intersection of programming and biology, sparking my interest in the world of computer science.

Upon graduating from college, I was determined to explore the realm of computer science. I began my journey by enrolling in introductory courses like CS50 and eventually embarked on the post-baccalaureate program at Oregon State University. In the past year, as a part of this program, I've enthusiastically participated in hackathons, created various personal projects, and dedicated myself to continuous learning, both inside and outside the classroom.

As I progress on this remarkable journey, I am dedicated to learning new technologies and honing my skills. My goal is to consistently learn in the field of computer science and contribute to its ever-evolving landscape.
